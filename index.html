About Us


Oceanic Origin Shipping (OPC) Private Limited is a trusted name in the maritime industry, committed to delivering reliable, efficient, and quality ship crew management services worldwide. Headquartered in Navi Mumbai, India, we specialize in sourcing and managing highly skilled and motivated seafarers to meet the rigorous demands of global shipping.

Driven by a passion for safety, professionalism, and customer satisfaction, we strive to exceed client expectations through innovation, teamwork, and a tradition of quality. Our comprehensive service offerings include crew supply, training, certification compliance, visa assistance, and more—ensuring operational excellence on every voyage.

At Oceanic Origin Shipping (OPC) Private Limited, we don't just manage crews—we empower them. By continuously investing in training and skill development, we prepare our personnel to uphold the highest standards of safety, efficiency, and environmental responsibility.

Your mission is our priority. Your success, our tradition.

What services does Oceanic Origin Shipping (OPC) Pvt. Ltd. provide?

We offer comprehensive maritime crew management services including:

  • Crew selection and recruitment
  • Personnel deployment and rotation
  • Documentation and certification management
  • Training and development programs
  • Compliance with international maritime standards (STCW, IMO, MLC)
  • Payroll and benefits administration
Where is Oceanic Origin Shipping (OPC) Pvt. Ltd. headquartered?

Our global headquarters is located in Navi Mumbai, Maharashtra, India. We have regional offices in:

  • Singapore (Asia-Pacific operations)
  • Dubai (Middle East operations)
  • Piraeus, Greece (European operations)
  • Houston, USA (Americas operations)
What kind of vessels do you supply crew for?

We supply qualified crew for all major vessel types including:

  • Oil Tankers: VLCC, Suezmax, Aframax, Product Tankers
  • Container Ships: Ultra Large, Post-Panamax, Feeder
  • Bulk Carriers: Capesize, Panamax, Handysize
  • Offshore Support: PSV, AHTS, FPSO, Drill Ships
  • Specialized: LNG/LPG carriers, Chemical Tankers, Reefer
How can I apply for a job as a seafarer?

We have multiple application channels:

  1. Online: Visit our careers portal and complete the application form
  2. Email: Send your documents to careers@oceanicorigin.com
  3. In-person: Visit our recruitment centers with:
    • Updated resume/CV
    • Certificates (STCW, COC, Specialized training)
    • Seaman book and passport copies
    • Medical fitness certificate

Our recruitment team will contact you within 5-7 working days.

Do you provide training for crew members?

Yes, we operate a state-of-the-art training center with:

  • Mandatory STCW courses
  • ISM Code familiarization
  • Vessel-specific orientation
  • Safety and emergency procedures
  • Advanced simulator training (ECDIS, GMDSS, etc.)
  • Leadership and management programs

We also partner with maritime academies for specialized certifications.

What is the process of selection and joining?

Our 5-step selection process:

  1. Document Verification: 1-2 days
  2. Technical Interview: Conducted by senior officers
  3. Medical Examination: Approved clinics worldwide
  4. Contract Finalization: Including salary, benefits, and terms
  5. Deployment: With travel arrangements and joining instructions

Average processing time: 7-14 days for urgent requirements.

Who do I contact in case of an emergency during my contract?

We provide 24/7 emergency support through:

  • Emergency Hotline: +91 22 1234 5678 (operational 24/7)
  • Email: emergency@oceanicorigin.com (monitored continuously)
  • Onshore Support: Local representatives in all major ports

Your joining documents will include specific contact details for your vessel/region. For medical emergencies, we have tie-ups with international assistance companies.

Privacy Policy

Oceanic Origin Shipping (OPC) Pvt. Ltd. ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of our seafarers, clients, and website visitors. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our services or visit our website.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Identification Data: Full name, date of birth, passport details, seaman book number
  • Contact Information: Address, email, phone number, emergency contacts
  • Professional Data: Certifications (STCW, COC), work history, training records
  • Medical Information: Medical fitness certificates, vaccination records
  • Financial Data: Bank details for payroll purposes, tax information

Non-Personal Information

We may collect anonymous usage data when you visit our website, including:

  • Browser type and version
  • Pages visited and time spent
  • IP address (anonymized)
  • Referring website

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • Crew management and deployment operations
  • Compliance with maritime regulations (STCW, MLC, ISM)
  • Payroll processing and benefits administration
  • Training and certification tracking
  • Emergency contact and medical assistance
  • Communication regarding employment opportunities
  • Website improvement and user experience
  • Legal compliance and regulatory requirements

3. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information with:

  • Shipping Companies: For crew placement on vessels
  • Maritime Authorities: For certification and compliance verification
  • Medical Facilities: For health assessments and emergency care
  • Training Centers: For certification and skill development
  • Government Agencies: As required by law (immigration, tax, etc.)
  • Service Providers: Third parties who assist in our operations (IT, payroll, etc.)

We do not sell your personal information to third parties.

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

  • Encrypted storage and transmission of sensitive data
  • Restricted access to personal information
  • Regular security audits and updates
  • Secure document disposal procedures

Despite these measures, no electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal information:

  • Active Seafarers: Throughout employment plus 7 years
  • Applicants: 2 years from last contact
  • Medical Records: As required by maritime law
  • Financial Data: 7 years for tax compliance

Data is securely deleted when no longer needed.

6. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the right to:

  • Access your personal information
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data (where applicable)
  • Object to certain processing activities
  • Request restriction of processing
  • Data portability (where applicable)

To exercise these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer.

7. International Data Transfers

As a global maritime crewing company, your data may be transferred to:

  • Countries where vessels are registered
  • Our regional offices worldwide
  • Client companies in different jurisdictions

We ensure adequate protections are in place for international transfers.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. The updated version will be posted on our website with a new "Last Updated" date.

Terms and Conditions of Employment

These Terms and Conditions outline the responsibilities, rights, and expectations of the Company and the Employee during the period of maritime employment.

1 Probation and Termination

The Employee will be on probation during the initial period of the contract.

The Company reserves the full right to terminate the contract at any time during or after probation.

If the Employee is found unqualified, incompetent, or lacking required ISM knowledge, only 50% of the agreed salary will be paid from the date of joining.

All costs incurred by the Company including air tickets, agency fees, and sign-on/sign-off charges will be deducted from the Employee's salary in such cases.

2 Job Assignment and Area of Operation

The Employee agrees to travel to the designated area of operations and work on any vessel assigned by the Company.

The Company has the right to change the vessel or operation as required.

3 Work Schedule and Hours

The normal working schedule will be 12 hours per day.

The Employee is entitled to a minimum of 10 hours of rest in any 24-hour period.

Work schedules are subject to change based on operational needs, emergencies, or unforeseen circumstances.

4 Duties Onboard

The Employee will perform duties onboard the vessel or on dockside if required, as part of normal seafaring activities.

All tasks must be carried out under supervision, in line with the Safety Management System, and with respect to maritime laws.

5 Shore Leave

Shore leave is granted only at the sole discretion of the Master or Company Agent, and based on operational, legal, and safety requirements.

6 Return Travel

The Company will provide return travel to India only after the contract is completed.

The route and mode of transportation will be decided by the Company.

7 Salary and Leave

The Employee will receive a monthly consolidated salary.

This salary includes leave compensation. No additional leave will accrue.

8 Taxation and Insurance

The Employee is responsible for paying income tax in their home country.

The Employee is responsible for maintaining valid social security insurance from their home country.

9 Bank Details

The Employee must ensure their bank account details are accurate and updated.

The Company will not be responsible for salary payment failures due to incorrect or outdated information.

10 Abandonment or Termination for Cause

If the Employee abandons the vessel, quits, or is terminated for cause, all payments will cease immediately.

The Company will not be responsible for any further compensation or repatriation costs.

11 Code of Conduct

The Employee must:

  • Behave courteously and professionally.
  • Avoid any actions that may damage the Company's reputation or property.
  • Follow all laws, client rules, and safety protocols.

Grounds for immediate termination include but are not limited to:

  • Alcohol/drug use
  • Theft or misconduct
  • Breach of confidentiality
  • Incompetence or gross negligence
  • Bringing unauthorized persons onboard
  • Misrepresentation of medical or personal information

12 Confidentiality

The Employee shall not disclose any confidential information regarding the Company, its operations, or clients.

13 License and Documentation

The Employee must possess and maintain all valid licenses, certificates, and documents required for their position at all times.

14 Agreement Acknowledgment

The Employee acknowledges having read, understood, and agreed to all the terms and conditions stated in this agreement.
